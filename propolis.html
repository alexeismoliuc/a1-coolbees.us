Propolis is a resinous residue from the first stage of bees digesting flower pollen. Bees use propolis to seal cracks and holes in the hive, thus protecting it from drafts and maintaining a special microclimate in it. With the help of propolis, bees regulate the width of the hive entrance depending on the ambient temperature. Bees polish the cells of the honeycomb with propolis before the queen lays eggs in them. When fairly large animals, such as mice, get into the hive and the bees are unable to pull them out of the hive, propolis is used by the bees to mummify the corpses, which prevents them from decomposing.
